From April 2026, the personal allowance stands at £12,570. However, for taxpayers with adjusted net income above £100,000, the allowance is progressively withdrawn — at a rate of £1 for every £2 of income above that threshold.

This creates an effective marginal tax rate of 60% on income between £100,000 and £125,140, before the standard 45% additional rate applies above that level.

From 1 April 2026, the National Living Wage rises to £12.21 per hour for workers aged 21 and over — an increase of 6.7% on the previous year.

For 18–20 year olds, the National Minimum Wage increases to £10.85, and the apprentice rate rises to £8.00 per hour.

Employers should audit their payroll before the April uprating date to ensure all affected workers receive the correct rate from day one.
